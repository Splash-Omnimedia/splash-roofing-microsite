Where Marketing Breaks Down

Marketing Gets Expensive When The Pieces Are Disconnected

Most roofing companies don't have a tactics problem — they have a connection problem. Tactics get added one at a time, each on its own, until the spend climbs faster than the results. Every breakdown below looks like a tactic issue on the surface and is a strategy gap underneath:

01

SEO Without A Clear Conversion Path

Traffic improves, but the visits don't turn into qualified roofing conversations. The site, offer, and tracking don't support the work.

02

Ads Without The Right Landing Page

Spend goes up, click volume looks fine, but the landing experience doesn't match the search intent or the buyer.

03

A Website That No Longer Reflects The Company

Your services, positioning, and team have grown. The site is still telling an older, smaller story.

04

Video Without A Sales Or Trust Strategy

You have footage, drone shots, project clips. None of it is doing real work in the sales process or website.

05

Leads Without Follow-Up Alignment

Marketing fills the top of the funnel, but the handoff to sales is slow, inconsistent, or undocumented.

What We Look At

What We Clarify Before Recommending Tactics

Strategy is not a survey or a kickoff slide. It's a deliberate look at six areas that decide whether marketing will land. Step through what we actually work through with your leadership team.

01

Business Goals

Where you actually want the roofing company to go next — so every marketing decision answers to a real target instead of a hunch.

What you walk away with

A clear growth target the entire marketing plan is built to serve.

Questions we work through
  • Where should growth come from over the next 12–24 months — revenue, crews, geography, or service lines?
  • Which jobs are most profitable, and do you want more of them?
  • What budget and timeline are we realistically working within?
  • What does "winning" look like to leadership, not just to marketing?
02

Audience & Service Mix

Who you want more of — and who you'd rather stop attracting — before a dollar goes toward reaching them.

What you walk away with

A prioritized audience and service focus marketing can actually target.

Questions we work through
  • Residential, commercial, or both — and where is the margin?
  • Repair, replacement, re-roof, or storm — which work should lead?
  • Which neighborhoods, property types, or accounts matter most?
  • Who are the wrong-fit leads quietly draining your team's time?
03

Market Positioning

What genuinely sets you apart in your service area — and whether that difference is actually showing up.

What you walk away with

A clear, defensible position that carries across every channel.

Questions we work through
  • Why do your best customers choose you over the company down the road?
  • Is that difference visible on the site, in ads, and in sales calls?
  • How do your top local competitors position themselves?
  • What proof backs up the claims you're making?
04

Website & Conversion Path

Whether your site turns interest into real conversations — or quietly loses people before they call.

What you walk away with

A conversion-focused website plan tied to how roofing buyers actually decide.

Questions we work through
  • Does the site reflect the company you've become?
  • Can different buyers — homeowner vs. property manager — find their path quickly?
  • Are the trust signals (reviews, work, credentials) doing their job?
  • Where are visitors dropping off before they ever reach out?
05

Visibility & Lead Generation

Which channels deserve your budget — and which are just noise — given your goals and market.

What you walk away with

A channel plan and budget priority matched to your goals and market.

Questions we work through
  • Where do your best leads come from today, and why?
  • Should SEO, LSAs, Google Ads, email, social, or video lead the mix?
  • What's the right balance of fast leads vs. long-term visibility?
  • Are you paying for activity that isn't producing the right calls?
06

Follow-Up & Measurement

Whether marketing activity actually becomes booked roofing work — and whether you can see it.

What you walk away with

A follow-up and reporting setup that connects spend to revenue.

Questions we work through
  • How fast and how consistently are leads followed up?
  • Where does the sales handoff break down?
  • What's actually tracked — calls, forms, booked jobs, revenue?
  • Do your reports tell you what to do next, or just what happened?
