The Real Problem

If People Cannot See The Quality Of Your Work, They Have To Guess

Roofing is a trust-driven business. Prospects want to know who they are hiring, what the work looks like, how the process feels, and whether your company is credible. Video helps answer those questions before the first call.

01

Finished Projects Are Not Being Shown

Work happens every week. Photos get taken. Video stays unshot, and prospects evaluate the company from text and a few static images.

02

Testimonials Are Not Being Captured

Customers say kind things after the job. None of those moments are recorded, and the trust they would create on the website, in ads, or in sales conversations sits unused.

03

Crews And Culture Stay Invisible

The team is professional. The work environment is strong. Prospects and potential hires never see it, and the company remains faceless online.

04

Website And Ads Lack Strong Visual Proof

Marketing campaigns run. Landing pages exist. The visual content is weak or generic, and the conversion rates stay low because the proof doesn't convince.

05

Video Content Is Not Planned For Reuse

A shoot happens. One video gets edited. The clips, b-roll, testimonials, and alternate cuts all sit unused when they could be supporting social, ads, email, sales, and recruiting.

Build A Smarter Video Plan
Strategy First

Video Should Start With The Marketing Strategy, Not The Camera

Roofing video should be planned around business goals, audience, service mix, website needs, paid campaigns, social content, sales enablement, recruiting, and proof gaps. The script, the shoot, and the edits all flow from what the company is trying to accomplish.

A strong shoot captures what the business needs next, not just what looks good in the moment.

  • What the video needs to accomplish
  • Which audience should trust the message
  • Which services and markets need visual proof
  • Which stories support residential vs. commercial buyers
  • Where the video will live
  • How clips will support social and paid ads
  • How testimonials will support sales conversations
  • How footage will be repurposed after the shoot
  • How performance will be measured

What Video Can Create

What A Roofing Video Library Can Include

Six kinds of video that roofing companies use to build trust, support sales, recruit talent, and show proof across multiple channels.

01

Brand Story Video

Introduce the company, leadership, values, service promise, and the reason customers should trust the team — usually 60 to 90 seconds, designed for the website and paid awareness.

02

Customer Testimonials

Capture real stories from customers, property managers, commercial buyers, or referral partners — in their own words, with genuine reactions that prospects can relate to.

03

Project Stories

Show the before, during, and after of residential or commercial roofing work with context and credibility — not just finished rooflines, but the problem, process, and outcome.

04

Drone & Jobsite Footage

Use aerial and jobsite visuals to show scale, quality, process, and professionalism — especially useful for commercial work and larger residential projects.

05

Short-Form Social Clips

Create vertical clips for Reels, Shorts, TikTok-style formats, Facebook, Instagram, and paid social — designed to stop scrolling and build familiarity.

06

Sales & Recruiting Assets

Support estimates, proposals, hiring, onboarding, culture, and follow-up with useful video content — clips that the team can actually use in real conversations.

Commercial Roofing Video

Channel Connection

Video Works Harder When It Has A Place To Go

Roofing video should not sit unused in a folder. Each clip should have a job across the website, paid ads, social media, email, sales, recruiting, and follow-up. The best shoots plan for that reuse from the beginning — not as an afterthought.

One piece of footage, cut for multiple contexts, can do more work than three separate shoots that each serve one channel.

Reporting

Measure Video By The Role It Plays

Views matter. What happens after the view — and whether the video helped a sale, a hire, or a campaign convert — matters more.

Visibility

Views, reach, impressions, watch behavior, and where videos are being seen — by platform, by channel, by content type.

Engagement

Clicks, saves, shares, comments, replays, and meaningful interactions — not just passive consumption.

Conversion Support

Website engagement, ad performance, form/call activity, email clicks, sales conversations, or recruiting inquiries influenced by video.

Asset Value

Which clips can be reused across pages, campaigns, emails, proposals, and social content — and which deserve more of the team's attention.
